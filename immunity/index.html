Immunity

Wed, May 21, 2025

What is immunity?

It is the ability to recognize foreign substances in our body, and our body tries to reduce them. It also destroys dead or damaged cells.It consists of extensive networks of cells and tissues.

Innate immunity

Has no memory so it reacts instantly and always by the same mechanism. It is mainly made up of white blood cells (monocytes) that constantly circulate in the blood to enter the blood vessels in the event of an inflammatory reaction. They move into the tissues and thus become macrophages (pathological waste eaters), which are made of foreign and dead cells (pathogens). This also includes the barrier function of the body, which is the skin, respiratory epithelium, gastric juices, etc. The skin secretes antibacterial latices in sebum. the respiratory epithelium produces mucus and helps remove debris from the airways. Gastric juices remove bacteria and toxins from ingested food.

Obtained immunity

It arises after previous contact with a foreign substance. It has a slower onset. There are two types of T and B lymphocytes. They originate in the red bone marrow, patella, skull, etc., but T- lymphocytes mature in the thymus and B- lymphocytes mature in the bone marrow. After contact with the pathogen, they start making antibodies. Immunological memory is formed, which later has a much faster and stronger response on contact with the pathogen.

How can you boost your immune system?

You can strengthen your immune system with diet, quality sleep and exercise

Food:

  • citrus- vitamin C
  • fish - vitamin D
  • minerals- maintaining levels (selenium and zinc etc.)
  • limit your intake of simple sugars

Sleep:

  • strengthens the immune memory
  • it’s crucial for the body’s regeneration
  • during deep sleep, immunoproteins are formed that stengthen immunity
  • make sure you get 7-9 of quality sleep

Exercise:

  • promotes circulation of immune cells
  • reducing stress and inflammatory processes
  • mild to moderate activity such as brisk walking, yoga or running
  • excessive training, on the other hand, can weaken immunity
